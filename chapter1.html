Q2: Cost of self-destructive coping strats

Linking to Q1 - display the possible costs for the selected coping mechanisms. All those strategies are simply pathways into deeper pain, offering only temporary relief. Note the strategies you use and their costs - and add any costs that you can think of on your own.

I spend a lot of time thinking over past errors and mistakes.
You might miss good things in the present, and regret missing them as well, compounding the problem. You might end up having depression about the past.



I use alcohol and drugs to numb myself.
You might get addicted, lose a lot of money, have problems at work, problems with relationships. Drugs and alcohol can also have serious health consequences.



I avoid dealing with the causes of my problems, like bad relationships.
You might put up with destructive relationships, or get burned-out doing work for other people. This can often lead to not having your own needs met, and depression.



I use food to punish myself, for example by either over or undereating.
You might have medical issues caused by weight gain, or issues such as bulimia or anorexia. Other consequences include feeling shame or embarrassment.



I avoid nice things, because I don’t think I deserve to feel good
This can lead to lack of enjoyment or lack of exercise, leading to depression, shame, or even depression.
